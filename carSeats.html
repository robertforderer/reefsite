Introduction



I found this geographic car seat dataset in R's ISLR package. It contains 400 different geographic regions with variables such as "advertising", "price", "population", and "sales". The goal of the decision-maker using this data would be to maximize "sales" using her understanding of variables like "price" and "advertising". My role as an analyst is to make interpretations, predictions, and recommendations to assist this hypothetical manager.



Variable Overview

As stated above, the goal of the decision-maker is to maximize "sales". Therefore, my interpretation of other variables is typically in-reference to "sales". The units for "sales" is in thousands of units sold. (The number 5 is more intuitively understood than 5,000) The average number of sales is 7.5, the standard deviation is 2.8, the maximum is 16, and the minimum is 0. (Surprisingly, only one geographic region exhibited zero sales.) The boxplot below demonstrates that most of the observations are clustered around the median while the first quartile extends to 0 and the fourth quartile extends up to approximately 15.
