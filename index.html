Data Story Telling: Interpretive and Predictive Models

Wine Quality Analysis

Our team analyzed the quality of wine from Portugal's Douro Valley using 11 chemical variables and R software. We used interpretive models like Decision Trees and Logistic Regression to interpret the chemical variables and predictive models like Random Forest and Quantitative Boosting to predict quality.

teamates: Katie Xia and Camille Zhu

Points per Game Regression Analysis

I decided to use Multiple Regression to better understand why certain NBA players score more than others. I started with eight variables and used stepwise regression to hone in on the best regression equation to describe the Points per Game statistic.

Car Seat Sales by Region

I used Data Mining Techniques and R software to better understand how to market Car Seats to different geographic regions. This analysis could help product design, marketing, and distribution decisions.

Logistics and Supply Chain: Linear Programming

NFL Schedule

Our team constructed a Linear Programming model to create an NFL schedule which minimized the distance traveled by all of the teams. Our 17,000 variable model resulted in a schedule which reduced total team travel by approximately 35%.

teamates: Joshua Tosches, Jacklyn Nguyen, Eric Li

Anadolou Efes Distribution Plan

Our team constructed a Linear Programming model to help drive facility location and distribution plans for a Turkish beer company, Anadolous Efes. Our model minimized the companies costs while meeting demand and production quotas.

teamates: Joshua Tosches, Jacklyn Nguyen, Eric Li

Paper Towel Distribution Plan

This project is similar to the Anadolou Efes Distribution Plan but contains much more detail. Our team helped a hypothetical paper towel company determine where to open stores and factories as well as determine a distribution plan. Our detailed model includes trucks which make multiple stops before returning to a factory.

teamates: Joshua Tosches
